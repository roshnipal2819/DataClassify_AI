# Laboratory Report 1444



## Introduction



This report documents the analysis of various oil-based samples using different laboratory instruments from Report_1444. Each sample group consisting of specific oil and additional compounds was subjected to multiple tests to determine chemical and physical properties. The samples were anonymized for analysis using various state-of-the-art techniques to extract relevant data about the mixture.



## Instrumentation and Methodology



Multiple advanced instruments were utilized to analyze the samples. Each instrument was selected to optimize the detection or measurement of specific properties identified as critical for the experimental objectives.



### Instrument List



1. **UV-Vis Spectrophotometer UV-2600**

2. **Thermocycler TC-5000**

3. **Rheometer R-4500**

4. **Centrifuge X100**

5. **Spectrometer Alpha-300**

6. **HPLC System HPLC-9000**

7. **Gas Chromatograph GC-2010**

8. **Microplate Reader MRX**

9. **Titrator T-905**

10. **Liquid Chromatograph LC-400**



### Sample Composition



Several mixtures were prepared for each set of experiments:



#### Sample Mixtures

- **Jojoba Oil, Beeswax**

- **Almond Oil, Cetyl Alcohol**

- **Coconut Oil, Gum, Glycerin**

- **Almond Oil, Beeswax, Glycerin**

  

## Results and Observations



### 1. **Analysis of Jojoba Oil and Beeswax**



#### Instrumental Measurements



#### Table 1: Analytical Data for Jojoba Oil and Beeswax

| Instrument                | Measurement Type            | Result   |

|---------------------------|-----------------------------|----------|

| HPLC System HPLC-9000     | Concentration               | 75 mg/L  |

| Spectrometer Alpha-300    | Wavelength                  | 320 nm   |

| Titrator T-905            | Molarity                    | 0.004 M  |



**Observations:**

- The concentration of the compound was recorded at 75 mg/L with the HPLC-9000.

- Measurements using the Spectrometer indicated a significant absorbance at 320 nm.

- The sample displayed a diluted molar strength at 0.004 M.



### 2. **Investigation of Almond Oil and Cetyl Alcohol**



#### Table 2: Analytical Data for Almond Oil and Cetyl Alcohol

| Instrument                | Measurement Type            | Result  |

|---------------------------|-----------------------------|---------|

| UV-Vis Spectrophotometer  | Absorbance                  | 2.7 Abs |

| Gas Chromatograph GC-2010 | Concentration               | 225 ppm |



**Observations:**

- The UV-Vis spectrophotometry showed strong absorbance characteristics at 2.7 Abs.

- Gas Chromatography results revealed a concentration level of 225 ppm, indicating a notable presence of target analyte.



### 3. **Characterization of Coconut Oil, Gum, and Glycerin**



#### Table 3: Analytical Data for Coconut Oil, Gum, and Glycerin

| Instrument                | Measurement Type            | Result     |

|---------------------------|-----------------------------|------------|

| Rheometer R-4500          | Viscosity                   | 245 Pa-s   |

| Microplate Reader MRX     | Optical Density             | 1.5 OD     |



**Observations:**

- Viscosity was determined to be 245 Pa-s as measured by the rheometer, suggesting a high resistance to flow.

- A Microplate reader revealed an optical density of 1.5 OD, crucial for assessing opacity or clarity.



### 4. **Details for Almond Oil, Beeswax, and Glycerin**



#### Table 4: Analytical Data for Almond Oil, Beeswax, and Glycerin

| Instrument                | Measurement Type            | Result   |

|---------------------------|-----------------------------|----------|

| Centrifuge X100           | Speed                       | 12000 RPM|

| Liquid Chromatograph LC-400| Concentration             | 78 ug/mL |



**Observations:**

- Centrifugation was performed at an intense speed of 12000 RPM, ensuring efficient separation of components.

- The concentration of target components was noted at 78 ug/mL, showing optimum solubility.



## Conclusion



The investigation through rigorous testing has demonstrated unique characteristics of each sample combination. Notably, the Almond Oil and Cetyl Alcohol mixture showed high absorption metrics, while Jojoba Oil and Beeswax exhibited distinct spectroscopic properties. This lab report presents substantial evidence for further exploration and possible applications of these mixtures in various commercial and pharmaceutical sectors.



The complexity of data and diverse instrumental approaches underline the multifaceted nature of analytical chemistry required to investigate such mixtures effectively.
