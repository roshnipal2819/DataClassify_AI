**Introduction:**



The objective of Experiment Series 1283 was to analyze various combinations of oil-based mixtures using advanced analytical instruments. Each test sample comprises specific combinations of ingredients which were subjected to rigorous testing procedures. The purpose was to observe and quantify different properties and behaviors of these mixtures.



**Test Sample Descriptions:**



Samples were prepared using combinations of oils and additives such as Coconut Oil, Beeswax, Vitamin E, Jojoba Oil, Gum, Glycerin, Almond Oil, and Cetyl Alcohol.



**Methodology:**



Each sample was subjected to various analysis techniques using a range of instruments including rheometers, spectrometers, and chromatographs. The testing involved assessment of viscosity, conductivity, molecular composition, and other critical parameters.



---



**Data and Observations:**



Table 1 provides an overview of the spectrometric and chromatographic analyses:



| Instrument             | Ingredients                                   | Measurement   | Unit      |

|------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------|-----------|

**Detailed Observations:**



* The spectrometric analysis for the Coconut Oil and Beeswax mix highlighted a specific absorbance peak at 650.2 nm, indicating a likely interaction between the oil and Vitamin E at this wavelength.  

* Rheological measurements identified a viscous behavior in the mix containing Jojoba Oil and Gum, recording a shear viscosity of 612.5 Pa-s. This behavior suggests structural interactions facilitated by the gum.
