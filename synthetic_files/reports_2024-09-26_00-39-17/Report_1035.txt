# Laboratory Report: Analysis of Oil and Additive Mixtures



## Report ID: Report_1035



### Introduction



This report encompasses detailed analyses of various mixtures containing oils such as Almond, Jojoba, and Coconut, combined with additives like Beeswax, Vitamin E, and others. Utilizing a range of state-of-the-art instruments, each mixture's unique chemical properties were meticulously examined to derive necessary conclusions. 



---



### Instrumentation and Methodology



A gamut of sophisticated devices was employed for this analysis, including but not limited to Mass Spectrometry, NMR Spectroscopy, and Ion Chromatography. Each device brings forward its distinctive ability to elucidate intricate details of molecular interactions within the tested mixtures.



---



### Results



The findings from our analysis are aggregated in the following tables, where each mixture is assessed through its corresponding measurement parameters.



#### **Table 1: Mass and Ion Spectrometry Analysis**



| Instrument               | Test Mixture                | Data          | Unit |

|--------------------------|-----------------------------|---------------|------|

| Mass Spectrometer MS-20  | Almond Oil, Beeswax         | 1200          | m/z  |

| Ion Chromatograph IC-2100| Almond Oil, Glycerin        | 20            | mM   |



During ion separation, Almond Oil and Beeswax were subjected to mass spectrometric analysis, resulting in a prominent m/z ratio of 1200. Conversely, using ion chromatography, the Almond Oil and Glycerin mixture demonstrated a concentration result of 20 mM.



---



#### **Table 2: Spectroscopy and Conductivity**



| Instrument                          | Mixture                     | Measurement | Unit   |

|-------------------------------------|-----------------------------|-------------|--------|

| FTIR Spectrometer FTIR-8400         | Jojoba Oil, Gum, Glycerin   | 2500        | 1/cm   |

| NMR Spectrometer NMR-500            | Jojoba Oil, Cetyl Alcohol   | 10          | ppm    |

| Conductivity Meter CM-215           | Jojoba Oil, Beeswax         | 1500        | uS/cm  |

| Spectrometer Alpha-300              | Jojoba Oil, Gum             | 800         | nm     |



Whilst employing an FTIR Spectrophotometer, key absorbance peaks were detected at 2500 1/cm, highlighting molecular bonds present in the Jojoba Oil, Gum, and Glycerin mixture. Moreover, NMR analysis revealed significant proton environment shifts at 10 ppm for Jojoba Oil with Cetyl Alcohol.



---



#### **Table 3: Additional Analytical Insights**



| Device                             | Combination                        | Reading   | Unit  |

|------------------------------------|------------------------------------|-----------|-------|

| Viscometer VS-300                  | Coconut Oil, Cetyl Alcohol         | 5147.98   | cP    |

| Titrator T-905                     | Almond Oil, Vitamin E              | 0.005     | M     |

| Microplate Reader MRX              | Almond Oil, Cetyl Alcohol, Vitamin E | 3.2     | OD    |

| PCR Machine PCR-96                 | Almond Oil, Beeswax, Vitamin E     | 28        | Ct    |



An intriguing viscosity value was determined at 5147.98 cP for the Coconut Oil and Cetyl Alcohol mixture. Titration procedures on Almond Oil with Vitamin E yielded a molarity of 0.005 M. The PCR amplification cycle threshold (Ct value) was determined to be 28, indicative of a notable concentration of initial template DNA in the Almond Oil, Beeswax, and Vitamin E mixture.
