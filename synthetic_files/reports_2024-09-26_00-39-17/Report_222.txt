## Introduction



In this lab report, we explore various mixtures of oil with other components such as gum, beeswax, and glycerin. The objective was to analyze these mixtures using different laboratory instruments to understand their properties and interactions. 



The samples tested included Almond Oil, Coconut Oil, and Jojoba Oil, each combined with variable ingredients. The labs were conducted with precision instruments including NMR Spectrometer, Ion Chromatograph, Gas Chromatograph, PCR Machine, UV-Vis Spectrophotometer, Viscometer, and Centrifuge.



### Random Information



Meticulous observations were also documented throughout the process, ensuring comprehensive analysis. The temperature of the lab was kept consistently at 22±1°C throughout the experiment, and LED lighting was used to minimize the influence of extraneous factors.



---



## Materials and Methods



1. **NMR Spectrometer NMR-500**  

    - **Sample**: Almond Oil, Gum; Almond Oil, Vitamin E  

    - **Parameters**: Chemical shift (ppm)

    

2. **Ion Chromatograph IC-2100**  

    - **Sample**: Coconut Oil; Jojoba Oil, Gum, Glycerin  

    - **Parameters**: Concentration (mM)

    

3. **Gas Chromatograph GC-2010**  

    - **Sample**: Jojoba Oil, Gum; Coconut Oil, Beeswax, Glycerin  

    - **Parameters**: Concentration (ppm)

    

4. **PCR Machine PCR-96**  

    - **Sample**: Almond Oil, Beeswax, Vitamin E; Coconut Oil, Cetyl Alcohol  

    - **Parameters**: Cycle threshold (Ct)

    

5. **UV-Vis Spectrophotometer UV-2600**  

    - **Sample**: Almond Oil, Beeswax, Glycerin  

    - **Parameters**: Absorbance (Abs)

    

6. **Centrifuge X100**  

    - **Sample**: Jojoba Oil, Cetyl Alcohol  

    - **Parameters**: Speed (RPM)

    

7. **Viscometer VS-300**  

    - **Sample**: Coconut Oil, Cetyl Alcohol, Vitamin E; Almond Oil, Cetyl Alcohol  

    - **Parameters**: Viscosity (cP)

## Results



The experimentation revealed significant variations in chemical interactions and physical properties across different oil-based mixtures. Notably, the NMR analysis of Almond Oil mixtures exhibited distinct ppm values, suggesting chemical interactions between the oil and other components. The UV-Vis technique highlighted varying absorbance characteristics, providing insight into potential turbidity or blend uniformity.



In-depth analysis of viscosity using the Viscometer showed differential resistance to flow, highlighting the rheological properties unique to each sample's composition.
