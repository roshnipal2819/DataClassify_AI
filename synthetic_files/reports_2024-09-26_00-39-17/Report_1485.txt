### Introduction

This lab report presents an extensive analysis of various test samples using different laboratory instruments. The aim is to investigate the rheological, thermal, tribological, chromatographic, and spectrometric properties of selected mixtures. Each group of ingredients forms a unique sample, and detailed observations, measurements, and results are documented.



### Materials and Methods



#### Instruments and Test Samples

Below is a summary of instruments used and the associated test samples:



- **Rheometer R-4500**  

   - Sample: Almond Oil, Beeswax  

   - Measurement: 450 Pa-s  

   - **Observation**: The viscosity of the almond oil and beeswax mixture was measured to be significantly high, indicating strong intermolecular interactions. This result is consistent with the properties of a semi-solid substance.



- **PCR Machine PCR-96**  

   - Sample: Jojoba Oil, Cetyl Alcohol, Vitamin E  

   - Measurement: 23 Ct  

   - **Observation**: The amplification cycle threshold (Ct) was determined, showcasing the efficiency of the PCR process for the compound mixture.



- **Four Ball FB-1000**  

   - Sample: Jojoba Oil, Beeswax, Glycerin  

   - Measurement: 0.850 mm  

   - **Observation**: The wear scar diameter reported reflects good tribological stability, suitable for lubrication purposes.



- **Liquid Chromatograph LC-400**  

   - Sample: Coconut Oil, Gum  

   - Measurement: 200 µg/mL  

   - **Observation**: Elution profile demonstrated distinct peaks, with a concentration of 200 µg/mL, indicative of a moderately concentrated sample.



- **Ion Chromatograph IC-2100**  

   - Sample: Coconut Oil, Beeswax  

   - Measurement: 10 mM  

   - **Observation**: Ion concentration was measured at 10 mM, suggesting proper ion exchange capacity of the mixture.



- **Mass Spectrometer MS-20**  

   - Sample: Almond Oil, Glycerin  

   - Measurement: 1500 m/z  

   - **Observation**: The mass-to-charge ratio revealed characteristic fragmentation patterns, useful for structural analysis.

### Discussion

The amalgamation of almond oil and beeswax demonstrated a high viscosity, aligning with their application in products like lotions and creams. The jojoba oil-mixture in the PCR test delivered an efficient cycle threshold, perhaps due to the stabilizing properties of cetyl alcohol and vitamin E. Notably, the wear analysis under tribological conditions for the combination of jojoba oil, beeswax, and glycerin resulted in minimal wear, suggesting potential for industrial lubricants.



In chromatographic evaluations, coconut oil and gum exhibited prominent peaks, likely due to inherent viscosities and density. The ion chromatographic results of coconut oil and beeswax reflected significant ion interaction, proving useful in emulsion and colloidal systems. Mass spectrometry provided intricate fragmentation insights for almond oil combined with glycerin, essential for understanding the molecular structure.



### Conclusion

Each mixture revealed distinctive profiles across multiple analytical techniques, offering insights beneficial for industrial applications. The diversity in physical and chemical attributes underscores the potential of these natural ingredients in various formulations. Further studies could explore temperature dependencies or additional compound interactions for broader applications.
