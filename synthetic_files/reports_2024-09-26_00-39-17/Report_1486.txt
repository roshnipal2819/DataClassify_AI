# Laboratory Report: Analysis of Various Oil-Based Mixtures



---



## Introduction



The aim of this study, which corresponds to Report_1486, was to investigate the physical and chemical properties of oil-based mixtures using multiple analytical techniques. The mixtures were derived from common oils and additives, such as Almond Oil, Jojoba Oil, Cetyl Alcohol, Beeswax, and others. An eclectic mix of state-of-the-art equipment was used to provide a comprehensive profile of these mixtures, including instruments like the Four Ball FB-1000, Centrifuge X100, and Viscometer VS-300, among others.



A focus was placed on evaluating parameters, such as viscosity, pH, conductivity, and others to better understand the potential applications of these mixtures. Even though centrifugal forces and magnetic properties were theoretically significant, they were not the primary focus of this investigation.



---



## Materials and Methods



### Test Equipment and Parameters



1. **Four Ball Tester FB-1000**:

   - Sample: Almond Oil with Cetyl Alcohol

   - Measurement: Wear Scar Diameter

   - Result: 0.750 mm



2. **Centrifuge X100**:

   - Sample: Jojoba Oil, Beeswax, Glycerin

   - Measurement: Spin Rate

   - Result: 12000 RPM



3. **Mass Spectrometer MS-20**:

   - Sample: Jojoba Oil, Gum, Glycerin

   - Measurement: Mass-to-Charge Ratio

   - Result: 1500 m/z



4. **Conductivity Meter CM-215**:

   - Sample: Jojoba Oil, Cetyl Alcohol, Vitamin E

   - Measurement: Conductance

   - Result: 1850 uS/cm



5. **PCR Machine PCR-96**:

   - Sample: Jojoba Oil, Glycerin

   - Measurement: Cycle Threshold

   - Result: 25 Ct



6. **Microplate Reader MRX**:

   - Sample: Almond Oil, Beeswax, Vitamin E

   - Measurement: Optical Density

   - Result: 2.5 OD



7. **Rheometer R-4500**:

   - Sample: Almond Oil, Beeswax, Glycerin

   - Measurement: Viscosity

   - Result: 450 Pa-s



8. **NMR Spectrometer NMR-500**:

   - Sample: Almond Oil, Gum

   - Measurement: Chemical Shift

   - Result: 10 ppm



9. **pH Meter PH-700**:

   - Sample: Jojoba Oil, Gum, Vitamin E

   - Measurement: pH Level

   - Result: 7 pH



10. **Viscometer VS-300**:

    - Sample 1: Almond Oil, Vitamin E

      - Result: 7477.11 cP

    - Sample 2: Jojoba Oil, Beeswax, Glycerin

      - Result: 2862.47 cP

    - Sample 3: Coconut Oil, Gum

      - Result: 5277.09 cP

## Discussion



The data presented herein depict a diverse array of measurements that collectively suggest that the combinations of oils and additives significantly alter their physical properties. Particularly of interest was the behavior of Jojoba Oil when combined with various substances, leading to differences in viscosity and conductivity. Such variations were clearly evidenced in the observations from the Centrifuge X100 and the Viscometer VS-300 results.



The integration of Vitamin E across several samples introduced interesting shifts in both optical density and viscosity, as noted in the Microplate Reader MRX and Viscometer VS-300 tables. The almond oil mixtures displayed distinct wear patterns during the Four Ball Tester examination, indicating potential applications in specialized lubrications.



Adverse effects were minimized despite the potential disruptions from extraneous equipment noise, ensuring the integrity of the PCR cycle threshold measurements and other pH evaluations, crucial for future biochemical applications.



Overall, the collated results demonstrate the nuanced interplay of these bio-oil mixtures' physical attributes, offering insight into future formulation optimizations.

## Conclusion



The multifaceted analysis of these oil-based mixtures underscores the importance of detailed examination using diverse instrumentation. The variability in viscosity, conductivity, and other parameters accentuates the necessity to tailor oil formulations for specific industrial applications.
