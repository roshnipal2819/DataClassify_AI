## Introduction



This report presents the detailed analysis and testing of various cosmetic formulations using advanced analytical instruments. Each formulation consists of distinct components and is assessed through different testing methodologies to derive a comprehensive profile of its properties. The aim is to characterize the samples' spectral, chromatographic, conductive, and physical attributes to provide insightful information about their compositions and interactions.



---



## Methodology and Observations



The analysis encompassed multiple sophisticated instruments, each contributing unique measurements pertinent to the samples' properties.



### Instrumentation and Conditions



- **Spectrometer Alpha-300**: Applied to identify spectral absorption, providing details regarding sample coloration and potential interactions. Wavelength set at 400 nm.

- **Liquid Chromatograph LC-400**: Utilized to determine concentration levels of the mixture, with sensitivity at 250 µg/mL.

- **Conductivity Meter CM-215**: Recorded electrical conductivity offering insights on ion presence and mobility within the mixtures.

- **NMR Spectrometer NMR-500**: Utilizes resonance frequencies to study molecular interactions and structure, measured at 15 ppm.

- **FTIR Spectrometer FTIR-8400**: Designed for molecular vibrational analysis - frequency recorded at 1800 1/cm.

- **Gas Chromatograph GC-2010**: Assessed volatile components and separation efficiency, focusing on concentrations at 500 ppm.

- **Ion Chromatograph IC-2100**: Used for ionic analysis, ionic concentration determined at 50 mM.

- **PCR Machine PCR-96**: Amplification cycles observed for quantitative analysis - recorded at 25 Ct.

- **Titrator T-905**: Evaluated chemical reactivity and molarity index, resulting in titration endpoint of 5 M.

- **X-Ray Diffractometer XRD-6000**: Structural analysis via diffraction studies, showcased at 90 °C.

- **Viscometer VS-300**: Measured sample viscosity, presented as complex dynamic indices (not exceeding 7631.21 cP).



### Analytical Observations



Each formulation demonstrated unique characteristics upon testing. The combination of oils with varying additional ingredients such as gums, waxes, and alcohols resulted in distinct behavior across different instruments. Molecular vibrations observed in FTIR and NMR analyses revealed significant interactions, enhancing the understanding of formulation stability and behavior.

### Discussion



1. **Spectrometry and Chromatography**: While all samples exhibited unique spectrometric signatures, chromatographic behaviors showed significant diversity in concentration levels. The Coconut Oil combination maintained consistent chromatographic profiles suggesting stable component interaction.



2. **Conductive and Molecular Analysis**: Notably, NMR results for mixtures containing alcohols reflected pronounced molecular interactions, while conductivity data revealed the ionic character of formulations, as seen predominantly in samples with 'Cetyl Alcohol'.



3. **Physical Properties**: Viscosity measurements demonstrated variability in physical texture, with samples including 'Gum' showing elevated viscosity, implicating potential structural contributions by its polymeric microstructure.
