# Laboratory Report 455



## Introduction



This report summarizes the results of multiple tests performed on different oil-based mixtures using a variety of analytical instruments. Each group of ingredients was treated as a unique sample and evaluated for specific properties pertinent to their composition and potential applications. The instruments used ranged from rheometers to X-ray diffractometers, each providing unique insights into the materials' properties.



## Methods and Materials



The following materials were used across different tests:



- Jojoba Oil

- Almond Oil

- Coconut Oil

- Gum

- Cetyl Alcohol

- Glycerin

- Beeswax

- Vitamin E



Each mixture was subjected to a series of instrumental analyses, recorded as follows:



### Instrumentation



1. **Rheometer R-4500**

2. **Ion Chromatograph IC-2100**

3. **Titrator T-905**

4. **Mass Spectrometer MS-20**

5. **UV-Vis Spectrophotometer UV-2600**

6. **X-Ray Diffractometer XRD-6000**

7. **Conductivity Meter CM-215**

8. **Spectrometer Alpha-300**

9. **Four Ball FB-1000**

10. **FTIR Spectrometer FTIR-8400**

11. **Viscometer VS-300**



## Observations and Results



### Rheological Analysis



**Sample Mixture: Jojoba Oil, Gum, Glycerin**



- **Instrument:** Rheometer R-4500  

- **Observation:** The consistency was homogeneous with a slightly viscous texture.  

- **Viscosity Measurement:** 25.6 Pa∙s  



### Chromatographic Analysis



**Sample Mixture: Almond Oil, Cetyl Alcohol, Vitamin E**



- **Instrument:** Ion Chromatograph IC-2100  

- **Observation:** The chromatogram displayed distinct peaks, indicative of each compound.  

- **Concentration:** 12.3 mM  



(Table 1: Summary of Chromatographic Data)



| Sample Mixture                         | Instrument                 | Measurement | Units |

|----------------------------------------|----------------------------|-------------|-------|

| Almond Oil, Cetyl Alcohol, Vitamin E   | Ion Chromatograph IC-2100  | 12.3        | mM    |



### Titration Analysis



**Sample Mixture: Coconut Oil, Gum**



- **Instrument:** Titrator T-905  

- **Observation:** The endpoint was sharp, demonstrating a clear titration curve.  

- **Concentration:** 5.7 M  



### Spectrometric Analysis



**Sample Mixture: Jojoba Oil, Beeswax**



- **Instrument:** Spectrometer Alpha-300  

- **Observation:** Peak absorbance observed at a wavelength specific to typical unsaturated compounds.  

- **Wavelength:** 675 nm  



### X-Ray Diffraction Analysis



**Sample Mixture: Jojoba Oil, Glycerin**



- **Instrument:** X-Ray Diffractometer XRD-6000  

- **Observation:** Crystalline structures detected with significant peaks.  

- **Angle:** 120 degrees  



### Mass Spectrometric Analysis



**Sample Mixture: Coconut Oil, Beeswax, Glycerin**



- **Instrument:** Mass Spectrometer MS-20  

- **Observation:** The mass spectrum revealed a prominent peak consistent with the glycerides present.  

- **m/z Ratio:** 1450

### UV-Visible Spectroscopy



**Sample Mixture: Coconut Oil, Cetyl Alcohol, Glycerin**



- **Instrument:** UV-Vis Spectrophotometer UV-2600  

- **Observation:** Absorbance value falls within expected range for viscous samples.  

- **Absorbance:** 2.8  



### Conductivity



**Sample Mixture: Almond Oil**



- **Instrument:** Conductivity Meter CM-215  

- **Observation:** Conductivity indicative of low ionic content.  

- **Conductivity:** 950 µS/cm  



### Four Ball Test



**Sample Mixture: Jojoba Oil, Cetyl Alcohol**



- **Instrument:** Four Ball FB-1000  

- **Observation:** Wear scar was minimal, indicating excellent lubricating properties.  

- **Wear Scar Diameter:** 0.450 mm  



### Infrared Spectroscopy



**Sample Mixture: Jojoba Oil, Gum, Glycerin**



- **Instrument:** FTIR Spectrometer FTIR-8400  

- **Observation:** Characteristic peaks corresponded with functional groups present in the mixture.  

- **Wavenumber:** 3000 1/cm  



## Discussions and Conclusions



The tests provided comprehensive insights into the chemical and physical characteristics of each sample mixture. The rheological properties varied significantly between mixtures, with Jojoba Oil, Gum, and Glycerin displaying typical viscous behavior. Chromatographic and spectroscopic data confirmed the presence and concentration of key components within the mixtures, supporting potential applications in fields such as cosmetics and pharmaceuticals. The minimal wear in the lubricating properties test suggests potential for industrial use.



In conclusion, each mixture presented unique attributes valuable for targeted applications, demonstrating the importance of instrument-based analysis in assessing complex mixtures. Further study could potentially address the scale-up challenges and explore interactions more deeply within the mixtures.
