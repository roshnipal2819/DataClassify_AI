**Observations:**



Certain oil mixtures exhibited unique properties under different tests. For instance, the **Viscometer VS-300** demonstrated how altering an ingredient's concentration marginally affected the viscosity, with Jojoba Oil mixtures showing notably high cP values.



Interestingly, in the irrelevant observation, a coffee spill was recorded during the Ionic Chromatograph operation, proving inconsequential but noted for comprehensive records.



Additionally, the **Spectrometer Alpha-300** data indicated a peak wavelength shift, implying potential interactions at the molecular level within Coconut Oil and Beeswax. This could be explored further through solvent-based extraction methods.

**Complex Insights:**



The interaction seen in the **Gas Chromatograph GC-2010** analysis of Jojoba Oil and Beeswax (with Glycerin) at 50.5 ppm suggests stability suitable for cosmetic formulation applications. The electrical conductivity of the Coconut Oil composite in the **Conductivity Meter** was unexpectedly high, indicating potential ionic charge presence, warranting further investigation into the homologous proportions of constituent additives.
