## Methodology

- Coconut Oil, Gum, Vitamin E

- Almond Oil, Beeswax



## Equipment and Measurements



### Analytical Equipment Details



1. **Microplate Reader MRX:** Used for optical density (OD) measurements at a wavelength determined suitable for the sample matrix.

2. **Rheometer R-4500:** Utilizes a controlled stress measurement for viscosity (Pa-s).

3. **Conductivity Meter CM-215:** Measures the sample's ability to conduct electric current (uS/cm).

4. **Thermocycler TC-5000:** Assesses temperature influences on sample stability (°C).

5. **Mass Spectrometer MS-20:** Identifies mass-to-charge ratios (m/z) for compound characterization.

6. **Gas Chromatograph GC-2010:** Quantifies volatile components (ppm).

7. **UV-Vis Spectrophotometer UV-2600:** Determines absorbance (Abs) for sample transparency studies.

8. **NMR Spectrometer NMR-500:** Provides chemical environment details (ppm).

9. **Viscometer VS-300:** Measures sample viscosity in centipoise (cP).

### Results



#### Observations and Descriptions



- **Almond Oil with Vitamin E** was analyzed using the Microplate Reader MRX, revealing an Optical Density of **2.8 OD**, characterized by its clear visual property.

  

- **Jojoba Oil Blend** subjected to Rheometric analysis displayed a viscosity of **450 Pa-s**, signifying considerable resistance to flow.

  

- The **Conductivity** of the Almond Oil and Gum mixture was remarkably high at **1200 uS/cm**, implying strong ionic activity within the solution.

  

- **Thermal analysis** conducted via Thermocycler TC-5000 confirmed that the Almond Oil, Beeswax, and Glycerin mixture stably maintained at **37°C**, showcasing potential in temperature-dependent applications.



#### Additional Measurements



Unrelated Findings:



- The Mass Spectrometric analysis of **Almond Oil and Vitamin E** mixture consistently yielded a mass-to-charge ratio peak at **550 m/z**, which aligns with predicted theoretical values.



- Through the application of Gas Chromatography, volatile substances within the Almond Oil and Cetyl Alcohol combination were detected at **45 ppm**.



- The **Coconut Oil, Gum, and Vitamin E** mixture exhibited an unusually high viscosity of **5245.89 cP**, measured via the Viscometer VS-300, indicating a remarkable thickness, potentially advantageous for creating creams or ointments.
